## 3. CEO Expectations

The CEO is expected to maintain the Annual Strategic Plan and Annual Operating Plan; drive Company strategy and execution; own customer, market, revenue, and partnership strategy; prepare budget and capital allocation recommendations with the finance owner; maintain operating cadence and governance records; surface Reserved Matters promptly; ensure decisions are documented in the Decision Journal; preserve Company continuity and long-term enterprise value; and keep Members reasonably informed of material developments.

## 4. CTO Expectations

The CTO is expected to own technical architecture and engineering execution; maintain source code, infrastructure, deployment, security, and technical documentation standards; steward AI architecture, model/provider implementation, and technical AI systems; maintain technical quality, reliability, scalability, and engineering velocity; document material technology decisions; identify technology risks that may become Strategic Technology Decisions; protect Company IP, secrets, and customer data; and maintain engineering and AI asset records appropriate to the Company's stage.

## 10. Availability and Leave

Each Founder should provide reasonable advance notice of material changes in availability, planned leave, reduced schedule, sabbatical, or outside commitment that may affect Material Participation. Approved Leave should be documented in writing with expected duration, coverage plan, compensation treatment, and vesting treatment if longer than six months.

## 11. Semi-Annual Review

Every six months, the founders should review responsibilities, objectives, time commitment, availability constraints, role clarity, support needed, compensation implications, whether officer assignments remain appropriate, and whether additional executive coverage is needed.

## 12. Performance Concern Process

If a Founder believes another Founder is materially failing to perform agreed responsibilities, the Founder should provide written notice identifying the responsibilities at issue, include supporting facts, propose corrective actions or support, meet within 14 days, provide a 30-day cure or improvement period unless otherwise agreed, and document the outcome.

Performance concerns alone do not affect vested equity unless the conduct separately qualifies as Cause, abandonment, or another Bad Leaver event.

## 13. Persistent Material Non-Performance

Persistent Material Non-Performance means unresolved material failure over a 12-month period to perform the responsibilities in this schedule after written notice, discussion, and a reasonable cure period. It may support removal from an officer role but does not by itself cause forfeiture of vested ownership.
