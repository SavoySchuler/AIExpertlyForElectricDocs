# Schedule D — Founder Expectations

Version: v0.2 draft  
Status: Founder review draft  
Related documents: `charter/FoundersCharter.md`, `legal/OperatingAgreement.md`, `schedules/FounderRolesSchedule.md`

## 1. Purpose

This schedule captures the founders' working expectations. It is designed to prevent expectation drift by making assumptions explicit. It should be reviewed at least semi-annually and whenever a founder's role, availability, compensation, or company strategy materially changes.

## 2. Operating Expectations

The founders expect to:

- preserve equal founding ownership while assigning clear operating authority;
- communicate material risks early;
- avoid surprise unilateral action on Reserved Matters;
- keep Company records current;
- maintain a written Decision Journal for major decisions;
- treat disagreements as governance issues before they become personal conflicts;
- separate ownership, employment, officer authority, and compensation;
- document role changes and leave expectations;
- avoid using information, approvals, access, or distributions as personal leverage.

## 3. Founder Meeting Cadence

| Meeting | Cadence | Purpose | Required Output |
|---|---|---|---|
| Founder Operating Meeting | Weekly | Execution, blockers, near-term decisions | Action items |
| Finance/Product Review | Monthly | Budget, customer, roadmap, risk review | Updated metrics and decisions |
| Member Meeting | Quarterly | Governance, approvals, strategy, capital allocation | Written minutes |
| Founder Review | Semi-annually | Roles, performance expectations, support, compensation | Updated Founder Roles Schedule |
| Strategic Planning | Annually | Mission, products, target customers, risk tolerance, hiring, budget | Annual Strategic Plan and Operating Plan |
| Governance Review | Annually | Documents, thresholds, decision matrix, policies | Governance update list |

## 4. Decision Expectations

Before escalating a disagreement, founders should identify:

1. the decision to be made;
2. the decision owner;
3. the applicable approval threshold;
4. relevant facts and assumptions;
5. business risks;
6. affected stakeholders;
7. whether the decision is reversible;
8. the cost of delay;
9. the proposed deadline for decision.

## 5. Communication Expectations

Founders should provide timely written notice for:

- inability to meet expected responsibilities;
- expected leave or reduced availability;
- material customer, security, financial, or legal issues;
- potential conflicts of interest;
- third-party acquisition or investment inquiries;
- proposed use of customer data beyond contracted rights;
- proposed Strategic Technology Decisions;
- material changes to compensation expectations;
- material outside commitments that may affect availability.

## 6. Conflict Norms

The founders agree to:

- raise conflicts early;
- document the business issue;
- use written proposals for major decisions;
- avoid withholding distributions, approvals, information, or access for personal leverage;
- provide business reasons when voting against Reserved Matters if requested;
- use the dispute process when informal discussion fails;
- preserve Company continuity during disputes.

## 7. Founder Health and Leave

Approved Leave may include parental leave, medical leave, caregiving leave, military service, bereavement, or other approved circumstances. Leave under six months should not affect vesting unless otherwise agreed. Longer leave must be documented with expectations for responsibilities, compensation, voting availability, vesting treatment, and return-to-work assumptions.

## 8. Workload and Compensation Expectations

If founder workload diverges materially, the founders should first address it through:

1. role clarification;
2. support or delegation;
3. salary adjustment;
4. bonus or milestone compensation;
5. future equity grants;
6. approved leave or reduced schedule.

Founder ownership should not be renegotiated solely because workload differs during a particular period.

## 9. Transparency Expectations

The founders should maintain shared access, subject to security rules, to:

- governance records;
- financial reports;
- Cap Table and Equity Ledger;
- major contracts;
- customer commitments;
- IP records;
- AI Assets Register;
- Decision Journal;
- security incident records;
- fundraising and acquisition materials.

## 10. Annual Expectation Reset

At least annually, the founders should explicitly answer:

- Are we still building the same kind of company?
- Are we optimizing for profitability, growth, venture-scale financing, or optionality?
- Are the current officer roles still correct?
- Are current compensation arrangements fair for current contribution?
- Are capital allocation and distribution expectations aligned?
- Do we need new executives, advisors, employees, or governance structures?
- Are our voting thresholds still practical?
- Are we documenting decisions well enough?
- Are we preserving customer trust?
