## 1. Purpose

This schedule records initial capital contributions, later founder contributions, and founder loans. It reinforces the principle that ownership, cash contributions, expense reimbursement, compensation, and loans are separate unless the Members expressly approve a different treatment.

## 3. No Mandatory Capital Calls

No Member is required to make additional capital contributions unless the Members approve a written amendment or written contribution agreement. A Member's failure to make voluntary contributions does not dilute ownership unless the Members separately approve an equity issuance under the Operating Agreement.

## 4. Contribution Classification

Any money or property provided to the Company by a Founder must be classified in writing as one of the following:

1. capital contribution;
2. founder loan;
3. expense reimbursement advance;
4. purchase of Membership Units;
5. gift or forgiven amount;
6. other approved classification.

If classification is unclear, the default treatment is founder loan only if a written promissory note exists; otherwise, the Company should obtain Member approval before treating the amount as equity, debt, or compensation.

## 5. Founder Loan Default Terms

Unless otherwise approved in writing, any founder loan must be documented by a written promissory note and will use these default terms:

| Term | Default |
|---|---|
| Interest Rate | Wall Street Journal Prime Rate minus 1%, or another legally permissible rate approved by Members |
| Maturity | TBD in note |
| Security | Unsecured unless approved under the Governance Matrix |
| Conversion | No conversion into equity without required approval |
| Repayment Priority | Subordinate to ordinary operating needs and third-party debt unless otherwise approved |
| Documentation | Written note signed by Company and lending founder |
| Prepayment | Permitted unless note states otherwise |
| Approval | Member Approval unless outside standard terms, then Protected Approval |

## 7. Equity Purchase vs. Loan

If a Founder provides funds as an equity purchase rather than a loan, the Company must document:

- valuation;
- Units issued;
- dilution impact;
- updated Cap Table;
- preemptive rights treatment;
- approval record;
- securities and tax review.

No founder loan converts into equity automatically.

## 8. Expense Reimbursement

Ordinary business expenses advanced by a Founder should be submitted with receipts and reimbursed according to Company policy. Expense reimbursement is not a capital contribution or founder loan unless expressly documented as such.

## 9. Non-Cash Contributions

Non-cash contributions, including equipment, software, domains, IP, data, designs, or services, should be documented with:

- description;
- contributor;
- agreed value, if any;
- whether ownership transfers to the Company;
- whether the contribution is licensed or assigned;
- tax treatment review;
- approval record.

## 10. Contribution and Loan Review

At least quarterly, the Company should review outstanding founder loans, unreimbursed expenses, and any undocumented founder advances to avoid later disputes.
