# Schedule B — Reverse Vesting and Repurchase Rights

Version: v0.2 draft  
Status: Founder review draft  
Related document: `legal/OperatingAgreement.md`

## 1. Structure

Founder Membership Units are issued immediately but remain subject to the Company's contractual repurchase right until vested.

This is intended to create reverse vesting: founders are owners on day one, but the Company is protected if a founder leaves early, abandons the business, or otherwise triggers repurchase rights.

## 2. Vesting Commencement Date

The vesting commencement date is the Company's formation date unless the Members unanimously approve a different date in writing.

## 3. Vesting Schedule

Each founder's initial Membership Units vest over **48 months** with a **12-month cliff**.

- Before month 12: 0% vested.
- At month 12: 25% of the founder's initial units vest.
- After month 12: the remaining 75% vests monthly in 36 equal monthly installments.
- At month 48: 100% of the founder's initial units are vested.

## 5. Repurchase Price for Unvested Units

Unless otherwise required by law or a signed issuance agreement, unvested units may be repurchased by the Company at the lower of original purchase price or nominal value.

## 6. Exercise Process

To exercise repurchase rights, the Company should:

1. identify termination or trigger date;
2. calculate vested and unvested Units;
3. confirm whether the departing Founder is Good Leaver or Bad Leaver;
4. approve exercise under the Governance Matrix;
5. send written repurchase notice;
6. pay applicable repurchase price;
7. update Cap Table and Equity Ledger;
8. update tax and accounting records.

## 7. Good Leaver Treatment

A Good Leaver keeps vested units. The Company may repurchase unvested units. The Company may also exercise any buyout option for vested units at Fair Market Value under the Operating Agreement.

## 8. Bad Leaver Treatment

A Bad Leaver forfeits or is subject to repurchase of all unvested units. Vested units may be subject to Company purchase at Fair Market Value less the discount specified in the Operating Agreement, subject to attorney review for enforceability.

## 9. Approved Leave

Approved Leave of six months or less does not pause vesting unless otherwise unanimously agreed in writing. Longer leave must be documented and should address responsibilities, compensation, vesting, return date, and governance participation.

## 10. Double-Trigger Acceleration

If the Company is sold and a Founder is terminated without Cause or resigns for Good Reason within the post-closing period stated in the Operating Agreement or transaction documents, remaining unvested Units may accelerate under the Operating Agreement.

## 11. No Performance-Based Equity Forfeiture

Subjective performance concerns do not cause forfeiture of vested ownership. Performance concerns may affect officer role, compensation, future equity grants, and operational authority, but not vested founder equity unless the conduct independently qualifies as Cause, Abandonment, or another Bad Leaver event.
