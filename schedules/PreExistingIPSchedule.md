## 4. Ryan Dixon Pre-Existing IP

This Article identifies intellectual property, business assets, and operating activities owned or controlled by Ryan Dixon ("Founder") or by Frontline Ready LLC, a Washington limited liability company of which Founder is a member and/or owner ("Frontline"), as of the Effective Date of the AIExpertly LLC Operating Agreement (the "OA").

All items listed in this Schedule, and all items owned by Frontline as of the Effective Date or hereafter acquired or developed by Frontline independently of the Company, are Excluded Assets. Excluded Assets are not assigned, licensed, contributed, or otherwise transferred to AIExpertly LLC (the "Company") under §10.1, §10.3, or any other provision of the OA, and are not subject to any present or future assignment, license-back, or "shop right" in favor of the Company unless separately agreed in a signed writing.

1. The Frontline Ready LLC entity itself, its membership interests, governance, and corporate existence;

### Part 5 — Future Frontline Development

Without limiting the foregoing, any software, code, integration, workflow, dataset, prompt, model, or other asset developed by or for Frontline after the Effective Date is also an Excluded Asset, provided that such asset is developed:

1. Using Frontline's own personnel, contractors, funds, equipment, or accounts (and not using Company personnel during their Company work time, Company funds, or Company-owned accounts); and

The fact that Frontline may license AIExpertly software for use in Frontline's business does not cause Frontline's own independent developments to become Company IP.

## 5. Exclusions Must Be Specific

A founder may not rely on generic descriptions such as "all prior code," "personal frameworks," or "AI ideas" unless the asset is sufficiently described to identify what is excluded from Company ownership.

## 6. License to Company

If a pre-existing asset is used in Company products, services, infrastructure, operations, customer work, or AI systems, the founder must specify whether the Company receives:

- an exclusive license;
- a non-exclusive license;
- a perpetual license;
- a revocable license;
- a royalty-free license;
- source-code access;
- modification rights;
- sublicensing rights;
- rights upon founder departure;
- rights after acquisition, merger, or conversion.

## 7. AI-Specific Asset Categories

The founders should specifically identify any pre-existing prompt libraries, agent workflows, RAG pipelines, datasets, synthetic datasets, fine-tuned models, model weights, embeddings, evaluation suites, benchmarks, automation scripts, customer discovery materials, and product prototypes.

## 8. Open Source and Third-Party Materials

Open-source or third-party materials should be listed if they are material to Company products or operations and could affect commercialization, proprietary ownership, source disclosure, customer commitments, security obligations, or model redistribution rights.

## 9. Prior Employer and Third-Party Restrictions

Founders should not contribute assets that are subject to prior employer ownership, invention assignment, confidentiality, non-solicitation, license restriction, government grant obligation, university policy, or third-party contract restriction without legal review.

## 10. Pre-Formation Work

Work created for the benefit of AIExpertly before formal execution of the Operating Agreement should be treated as assigned to the Company unless expressly listed as excluded in this schedule and approved by the Members.
