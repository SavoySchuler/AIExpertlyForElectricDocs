Version: v0.2 draft  
Status: Founder review draft  
Related documents: `legal/OperatingAgreement.md`, `manuals/GovernanceManual.md`, `templates/DecisionJournalTemplate.md`

## Legend

| Approval Level | Meaning |
|---|---|
| CEO Authority | CEO may decide within approved budget, approved plans, and ordinary-course business authority |
| Domain Executive Authority | Responsible executive may decide within assigned domain authority, approved budgets, and approved plans |
| Member Approval | Approval by Members holding more than 50% of outstanding voting interests |
| Protected Approval | At least 75% of voting interests and approval of each original Founder while all three original Founders remain Members |
| Unanimous Approval | Approval of all voting Members |

Silence is not approval. Abstention is not approval. Approval thresholds are measured against outstanding voting interests unless the Operating Agreement expressly states otherwise.

## 9. Notice and Voting Windows

| Matter | Notice | Voting Window |
|---|---:|---:|
| Regular meeting | 14 calendar days | As stated in notice |
| Special meeting | 5 business days | As stated in notice |
| Routine Member matter | 5 business days | At least 7 calendar days |
| Reserved Matter | 14 calendar days | At least 14 calendar days |
| Strategic transaction | 30 calendar days where practical | At least 30 calendar days where practical |
| Acquisition offer | Within 5 business days after receipt | At least 30 calendar days unless external timing requires faster action |
| Emergency matter | As much notice as practical | Practical window based on circumstances |

## 10. Required Materials for Reserved Matters

A Reserved Matter proposal should include:

- decision requested;
- approval threshold;
- business rationale;
- financial impact;
- legal or compliance impact;
- customer impact;
- technology or AI impact, if applicable;
- alternatives considered;
- risks and mitigations;
- implementation plan;
- voting deadline.

## 11. Annual Review

Dollar thresholds and approval levels should be reviewed annually and updated by the required approval level. Changes that affect founder protections, ownership, voting rights, vesting, buy-sell rights, deadlock procedures, or constitutional provisions require Unanimous Approval.
