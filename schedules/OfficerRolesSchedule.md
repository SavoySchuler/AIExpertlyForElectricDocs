# Schedule G — Officer Roles, Authority, and Successors

Version: v0.2 draft  
Status: Founder review draft

> This schedule is intended to be updated as the Company grows without rewriting the core Operating Agreement. It describes expected domains, not guaranteed titles or employment rights.

## 1. Design Principles

1. Authority follows expertise.
2. Accountability follows organizational hierarchy.
3. Domain executives decide ordinary-course domain matters within approved budgets and plans.
4. Cross-domain, high-risk, ownership, capital, or strategy decisions escalate under the Governance Matrix.
5. Removal from an officer role does not remove Membership, vested equity, or voting rights unless the Operating Agreement separately provides otherwise.

## 2. Executive Role Summary

| Role | Primary Mission | Default Reports To |
|---|---|---|
| CEO | Maximize enterprise value and overall Company execution | Members |
| CTO | Deliver scalable, secure, maintainable technology | CEO |
| COO | Scale internal operations, process, and delivery | CEO |
| CFO | Own finance, accounting, treasury, tax, planning, and fundraising support | CEO |
| CIO | Own internal technology, identity, business systems, and enterprise IT | CEO or COO |
| CAIO | Maximize AI advantage through responsible AI governance and evaluation | CTO |
| CPO | Own product vision, roadmap, requirements, and customer value | CEO |
| CXO | Own customer experience, onboarding, support, retention, and advocacy | CEO or COO |
| CBO | Own partnerships, business development, licensing, and strategic growth | CEO |

## 3. Chief Executive Officer (CEO)

### Mission

Maximize sustainable enterprise value while executing the Annual Strategic Plan and Annual Operating Plan.

### Core Responsibilities

- Company strategy and execution.
- Revenue, sales, marketing, and partnerships.
- Capital allocation within approved budgets.
- Cross-functional prioritization.
- Organizational design and hiring priorities.
- Material customer and partner relationships.
- Member reporting and governance administration.

### Authority

The CEO may decide ordinary-course business matters within approved plans and budgets. The CEO may not unilaterally approve Reserved Matters, change ownership rights, issue equity, incur debt outside thresholds, sell Company assets outside ordinary course, or materially deviate from approved plans.

### Accountability

The CEO is accountable to the Members for overall Company performance, transparency, and execution.

## 4. Chief Technology Officer (CTO)

### Mission

Deliver secure, scalable, reliable, maintainable technology that supports the Company's business strategy.

### Core Responsibilities

- Software architecture.
- AI architecture and inference architecture.
- Engineering organization and development methodology.
- Security engineering and reliability.
- Cloud architecture and infrastructure.
- Engineering toolchain and technical standards.
- Technical roadmap execution.

### Authority

The CTO may decide ordinary technical implementation matters within approved budgets, including commercial LLM provider choice, vector database, model routing, frameworks, programming languages, CI/CD, monitoring, prompt orchestration, RAG implementation, and cloud tooling.

### Escalation

Technology decisions escalate when they constitute Strategic Technology Decisions, exceed delegated financial authority, materially affect another executive domain, materially alter Company risk, or involve sale/exclusive licensing of Core Technology.

## 5. Chief Product Officer (CPO)

### Mission

Define and deliver products that create durable customer value.

### Core Responsibilities

- Product vision and roadmap.
- Customer research.
- Feature prioritization.
- UX and product requirements.
- Product success metrics.
- Competitive product positioning.

### Authority

The CPO owns what the Company builds, subject to Company strategy, technical feasibility, approved budgets, and the CEO's cross-functional priorities.

## 6. Chief AI Officer (CAIO)

### Mission

Maximize the Company's competitive advantage through effective, responsible, measurable AI usage.

### Core Responsibilities

- AI governance and responsible AI practices.
- Model evaluation and benchmarking.
- Prompt engineering standards.
- AI experimentation and observability.
- AI safety and risk assessment.
- Internal AI enablement.
- Documentation of model provenance and AI assets.

### Authority

The CAIO recommends and administers AI standards within the technology organization. Unless otherwise approved, the CAIO reports to the CTO and does not independently set Company strategy or override the CTO's architecture authority.

## 7. Chief Operating Officer (COO)

### Mission

Translate strategy into reliable operating systems, processes, and delivery execution.

### Core Responsibilities

- Internal operations.
- Process design and scaling.
- Delivery operations.
- Vendor operations.
- HR operations and administrative operations.
- Legal operations coordination.
- Operating cadence and management systems.

## 8. Chief Financial Officer (CFO)

### Mission

Protect and optimize the Company's financial position.

### Core Responsibilities

- Finance and accounting.
- Treasury and cash management.
- Tax coordination.
- Financial planning and analysis.
- Budgeting and forecasting.
- Fundraising support.
- Investor reporting.
- Financial controls.

## 9. Chief Information Officer (CIO)

### Mission

Operate secure and effective internal technology systems.

### Core Responsibilities

- Internal IT.
- Identity and access management.
- Business applications.
- Employee devices and productivity systems.
- Enterprise security systems.
- Compliance systems.

### Distinction From CTO

The CTO builds and operates product technology. The CIO operates internal business technology.

## 10. Chief Experience Officer (CXO)

### Mission

Ensure customers receive value, support, retention, and a high-quality end-to-end experience.

### Core Responsibilities

- Customer onboarding.
- Support operations.
- Retention and customer health.
- Customer advocacy.
- Voice of customer.
- NPS or equivalent experience metrics.

## 11. Chief Business Officer (CBO)

### Mission

Drive strategic growth through partnerships, alliances, licensing, and business development.

### Core Responsibilities

- Strategic partnerships.
- Business development.
- Licensing opportunities.
- Corporate development support.
- Ecosystem relationships.
- Revenue operations coordination.

## 12. Succession and Coverage

If the CEO role is vacant, the Members shall appoint an interim CEO within 30 days. If another officer role is vacant, the CEO may assign interim coverage within approved authority. Critical functions should have at least one named backup once the Company has sufficient personnel.

## 13. KPIs and Expectations

KPIs are non-binding operating tools unless expressly incorporated into a compensation plan, employment agreement, or Founder Roles Schedule. Failure to meet KPIs may inform performance review, compensation, or officer role decisions, but does not affect vested equity unless the conduct separately qualifies as Cause or a Bad Leaver event.
