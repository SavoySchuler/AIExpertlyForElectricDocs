# AIExpertly Governance Playbook

Version: v0.2 draft  
Status: Founder review draft

> This playbook turns the Governance System into repeatable actions. It is designed for founders and future operators who need to know what to do when key governance events occur.

## 1. Purpose

The playbook converts governance rules into workflows. The Operating Agreement answers who has authority. The playbook answers what to do next.

## 2. Annual Planning Playbook

1. CEO circulates draft Annual Strategic Plan and Annual Operating Plan.
2. CFO or finance owner circulates budget, runway, cash, and reserve analysis.
3. CTO circulates technology roadmap, infrastructure risks, security risks, and Strategic Technology Decision candidates.
4. CPO circulates product roadmap, customer evidence, market findings, and roadmap tradeoffs.
5. CAIO circulates AI capability, model evaluation, model provenance, AI safety, and customer data usage review.
6. CXO circulates customer health, onboarding, retention, and support review.
7. CBO circulates partnerships, business development, licensing, and ecosystem opportunities.
8. Members meet to discuss strategy and operating assumptions.
9. Revisions are documented.
10. Members vote within the required voting window.
11. Approved plans are archived with effective date.
12. Decision Journal is updated.

## 3. Quarterly Governance Review

### Agenda

- financial results;
- budget variance;
- revenue and pipeline;
- product progress;
- technology and AI risks;
- security incidents;
- customer health;
- hiring and compensation;
- cap table changes;
- AI Assets Register changes;
- Reserved Matters;
- Decision Journal review;
- action items.

### Outputs

- minutes;
- decisions;
- owners;
- due dates;
- risks to escalate;
- schedule updates;
- policy updates.

## 4. Founder Review Playbook

1. Each Founder completes self-assessment.
2. Each Founder identifies support needed.
3. Founders review current Founder Roles Schedule.
4. Founders discuss time commitment, role clarity, workload, compensation, burnout risk, and expected availability.
5. Concerns are written clearly and factually.
6. Role updates are documented.
7. Compensation or support recommendations are escalated if needed.
8. Persistent issues are tracked without threatening vested equity unless they become Bad Leaver events.
9. Founder Roles Schedule is updated or expressly reaffirmed.

## 5. Reserved Matter Playbook

A proposal for a Reserved Matter should include:

- title;
- proposer;
- decision requested;
- approval threshold;
- business rationale;
- financial impact;
- legal or compliance impact;
- customer impact;
- team impact;
- risks;
- alternatives considered;
- implementation plan;
- deadline;
- documents to review.

### Process

1. Provide required notice.
2. Open voting window.
3. Allow questions and written explanations.
4. Record vote.
5. Archive decision.
6. Update relevant schedules or policies.
7. Add Decision Journal entry.

## 6. Deadlock Playbook

1. Confirm the decision is actually a Reserved Matter.
2. Identify approval threshold.
3. Require written business rationale from each Founder if requested.
4. Determine cost of delay and reversibility.
5. Negotiate in good faith for 30 days.
6. Engage mediator if unresolved.
7. Proceed to arbitration if mediation fails.
8. Use shotgun clause only for unresolved ownership or control deadlock after prior steps.
9. If buyout cannot be financed, evaluate third-party sale process.
10. Record all steps in Decision Journal.

## 7. New Member Playbook

Before admitting a new Member:

- define purpose of admission;
- prepare proposed economics;
- prepare governance rights;
- update pro forma cap table;
- disclose dilution;
- confirm preemptive rights;
- complete IP assignment and confidentiality agreements;
- confirm tax implications;
- approve under Governance Matrix;
- update Cap Table and equity ledger;
- update tax and accounting records;
- update governance contact list and notice records.

## 8. Equity Grant Playbook

For employee, advisor, or contractor equity:

1. Confirm whether an equity incentive plan exists.
2. Confirm remaining available pool.
3. Determine instrument type, such as LLC profits interest or another approved mechanism.
4. Prepare valuation support if needed.
5. Define vesting schedule.
6. Confirm service expectations.
7. Confirm IP, confidentiality, and invention assignment documents are signed.
8. Obtain required approval.
9. Execute grant documents.
10. Update Cap Table and equity ledger.
11. Track vesting.
12. Calendar repurchase, exercise, or expiration deadlines if applicable.

## 9. Founder Departure Playbook

1. Classify departure: Good Leaver, Bad Leaver, death, disability, resignation, removal, abandonment, or mutual separation.
2. Determine vested and unvested units.
3. Exercise Company repurchase rights for unvested units if applicable.
4. Determine whether Company will purchase vested units.
5. If Company declines, offer option to remaining Founders or Members.
6. Conduct valuation process if price is not agreed.
7. Document payment terms.
8. Revoke access and recover Company property.
9. Rotate credentials, API keys, signing authority, and bank permissions.
10. Confirm confidentiality and non-solicit obligations.
11. Update Cap Table, tax records, bank permissions, systems access, and governance contact list.

## 10. Abandonment Playbook

1. Identify responsibilities not being performed.
2. Confirm absence is not Approved Leave.
3. Document 90 consecutive days of material non-performance.
4. Send written notice with facts and cure actions.
5. Provide 30-day cure period.
6. Meet to discuss cure plan.
7. Offer support or role adjustment if appropriate.
8. If uncured, classify under Bad Leaver procedures.
9. Preserve evidence and follow dispute procedures if contested.

## 11. Acquisition Offer Playbook

1. CEO discloses offer within five business days.
2. Preserve confidentiality.
3. Share material documents with Members.
4. Confirm whether offer is bona fide.
5. Identify buyer, price, structure, timing, conditions, contingencies, escrows, indemnities, employee treatment, IP treatment, and customer impact.
6. Engage legal and financial advisors if appropriate.
7. Provide at least 30 days for review unless offer timing requires faster action.
8. Prepare alternatives analysis: reject, negotiate, raise, continue independently, seek other buyers.
9. Vote under Governance Matrix.
10. If approved, coordinate drag-along obligations and closing process.
11. Record rationale and alternatives in Decision Journal.

## 12. Fundraising Playbook

1. Define fundraising need and use of proceeds.
2. Confirm entity structure and investor expectations.
3. Prepare cap table impact.
4. Confirm preemptive rights.
5. Review investor governance rights.
6. Evaluate whether conversion to corporation is required.
7. Review tax, securities, and employment implications.
8. Approve under Governance Matrix.
9. Execute financing documents.
10. Update governance records and Cap Table.

## 13. Strategic Technology Decision Playbook

Escalate a technology decision when it commits material capital, materially changes customer data usage, releases or sells core IP, enters regulated markets, creates long-term irreversible infrastructure commitments, or changes business model or risk profile.

### Process

1. CTO prepares technical analysis.
2. CFO prepares financial analysis.
3. CEO prepares business rationale.
4. CAIO prepares AI governance impact if applicable.
5. CPO prepares product/customer impact if applicable.
6. CXO prepares customer success impact if applicable.
7. Members review and vote if required.
8. Decision is recorded.

## 14. Security Incident Playbook

1. Identify incident owner.
2. Contain incident.
3. Preserve evidence.
4. Assess affected systems, data, customers, and legal obligations.
5. Notify CEO, CTO, CIO, and legal advisor as appropriate.
6. Determine customer notice obligations.
7. Determine regulatory notice obligations.
8. Remediate vulnerabilities.
9. Conduct post-incident review.
10. Update Security Policy.
11. Record incident in governance records.

## 15. Customer Data Exception Playbook

Any request to use customer data outside ordinary service delivery must include:

- customer contract authority;
- purpose;
- data categories;
- anonymization or aggregation plan;
- retention period;
- model training impact;
- third-party sharing;
- security controls;
- customer notice or consent plan;
- legal review;
- required approval.

No customer data exception should proceed without written confirmation that the use is permitted by contract, policy, and law.

## 16. Operating Reserve and Distribution Playbook

1. CFO or finance owner calculates current cash.
2. Deduct current liabilities.
3. Deduct tax obligations.
4. Deduct approved operating reserve.
5. Deduct obligations under Annual Operating Plan.
6. Identify reasonably anticipated strategic commitments.
7. Calculate Excess Cash.
8. CEO prepares recommendation.
9. Members review if distribution is outside default policy.
10. Record decision and update financial records.

## 17. Founder Loan Playbook

1. Define loan purpose.
2. Confirm Company need.
3. Confirm no mandatory capital call is being created.
4. Prepare written promissory note.
5. Confirm interest rate and repayment terms.
6. Confirm subordination.
7. Confirm no equity conversion unless separately approved.
8. Approve under Governance Matrix.
9. Record in Founder Loans Schedule.
10. Track repayment.

## 18. Approved Leave Playbook

1. Founder requests leave or Company identifies need for leave.
2. Confirm leave category.
3. Document expected duration.
4. Identify temporary coverage for responsibilities.
5. Document effect on compensation.
6. Confirm vesting treatment if leave may exceed six months.
7. Set check-in cadence.
8. Update Founder Roles Schedule if needed.
9. Revisit before return date or extension.

## 19. Officer Transition Playbook

1. Identify transition reason: resignation, removal, restructuring, leave, hiring, or succession.
2. Confirm approval threshold.
3. Assign interim coverage.
4. Transfer systems access and signing authority.
5. Update Officer Roles Schedule.
6. Communicate internally and externally as appropriate.
7. Document transition in Decision Journal.

## 20. Governance Hygiene Checklist

Review quarterly:

- Cap Table current?
- Equity Ledger current?
- Decision Journal current?
- AI Assets Register current?
- Bank permissions current?
- System access current?
- Founder Roles Schedule current?
- Officer Roles Schedule current?
- Insurance reviewed?
- IP assignments complete?
- Contractor agreements complete?
- Customer data exceptions documented?
