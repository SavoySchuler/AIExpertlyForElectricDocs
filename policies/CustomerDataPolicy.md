# AIExpertly Customer Data Policy

Version: v0.2 draft  
Status: Founder review draft

> This policy governs customer data rights, restrictions, retention, AI usage, model training, benchmarking, third-party sharing, and customer data exceptions.

## 1. Purpose

Customer trust is a strategic asset. This policy establishes default rules for customer data so that AIExpertly does not accidentally use, disclose, train on, benchmark with, or commercialize customer data beyond the rights granted by customer agreements, law, and Company policy.

## 2. Core Rule

Customer data belongs to the customer or is controlled by the customer agreement. AIExpertly may use customer data only for approved service delivery and other uses expressly permitted by contract, law, and Company policy.

## 3. Covered Data

Customer data includes:

- customer-provided data;
- customer documents;
- uploaded files;
- user prompts;
- user outputs;
- support tickets;
- telemetry tied to customer accounts;
- customer configuration data;
- customer credentials or secrets;
- customer contact lists;
- customer usage data;
- customer-specific embeddings, vectors, indexes, or derived data;
- customer-specific model outputs.

## 4. Default Prohibited Uses

Unless expressly approved and contractually permitted, customer data may not be used to:

- train general-purpose models;
- fine-tune models for unrelated customers;
- benchmark third-party models;
- create commercial datasets;
- improve unrelated customer products;
- publish examples, demos, case studies, or screenshots;
- share with third parties beyond service delivery;
- test unmanaged AI tools;
- populate public prompts;
- create reusable customer-derived prompt libraries.

## 5. Approved Ordinary Uses

Customer data may be used for ordinary service delivery when consistent with contract and policy, including:

- operating the contracted product or service;
- providing support;
- debugging customer-specific issues;
- maintaining security;
- generating customer-requested outputs;
- maintaining customer-specific configurations;
- complying with legal obligations;
- preventing abuse or misuse.

## 6. AI Use Restrictions

Customer data may be submitted to AI tools or model providers only when:

1. the customer agreement permits the use;
2. the tool or provider has acceptable data retention and training terms;
3. the use is consistent with the AI Governance Policy;
4. the data classification allows the use;
5. required approvals have been obtained.

Sensitive customer data should not be submitted to unmanaged AI tools.

## 7. Customer Data Exceptions

Any proposed use outside ordinary service delivery requires a Customer Data Exception record covering:

- customer contract authority;
- purpose;
- data categories;
- anonymization or aggregation method;
- retention period;
- deletion plan;
- model training or evaluation impact;
- third-party sharing;
- security controls;
- customer notice or consent;
- legal review;
- required approval.

## 8. Anonymized and Aggregated Data

Anonymized or aggregated data may be used only if the data is reasonably de-identified, not reasonably linkable to a customer or individual, and permitted by customer agreement and law.

Anonymization must not be used as a workaround for contractual restrictions.

## 9. Retention and Deletion

Customer data should be retained only as long as needed for approved purposes, legal obligations, security, support, or contract requirements.

Deletion requests should be tracked, verified, and documented. Where deletion is not technically feasible due to backups or logs, access should be restricted and data should age out under the applicable retention schedule.

## 10. Access Control

Access to customer data should follow least privilege. Customer data access should be limited to personnel with a legitimate business need.

Access to sensitive customer data should be logged where practical and reviewed periodically.

## 11. Customer Data in Development and Testing

Production customer data should not be copied into development or testing environments unless approved, minimized, protected, and contractually permitted.

Synthetic or anonymized data should be used where practical.

## 12. Third-Party Sharing

Customer data may be shared with vendors or subprocessors only when:

- permitted by contract;
- reviewed for security and confidentiality;
- covered by appropriate data processing terms;
- necessary for service delivery or another approved purpose;
- documented in vendor records.

## 13. Customer Data Incidents

Unauthorized access, disclosure, loss, misuse, or prohibited AI usage of customer data is a security incident and must be handled under the Information Security Policy and AI Governance Policy.

## 14. Review Cadence

This policy should be reviewed at least annually and before enterprise customer commitments, regulated-market entry, major AI architecture changes, or material customer data usage changes.
