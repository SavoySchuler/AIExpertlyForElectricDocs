## Article I — Purpose, Principles, and Governance Philosophy

### 1.1 Purpose

This Operating Agreement establishes the ownership, governance, management, economic, intellectual property, dispute resolution, and operational framework of AI Expertly for Electric LLC (the **Company**). The Members adopt this Agreement to create a durable founder-led company capable of surviving growth, leadership change, Member departure, future financing, strategic pivots, acquisition, conversion, and dissolution.

### 1.2 Guiding Principles

When this Agreement grants discretion or requires interpretation, the Members intend that decisions be guided by the following principles:

1. Preserve the long-term health and continuity of the Company.
2. Maximize sustainable enterprise value.
3. Protect the Company before any individual Member.
4. Treat Members fairly and consistently.
5. Promote transparency, candor, and timely communication.
6. Resolve disagreements in good faith whenever reasonably possible.

These principles guide interpretation but do not override explicit provisions.

### 1.3 Ownership Philosophy

Ownership represents long-term partnership, founding risk, economic participation, and governance participation. Compensation represents current services. Ownership shall not fluctuate solely because one Founder temporarily works more or fewer hours, changes title, receives different compensation, or takes Approved Leave, except as expressly provided in this Agreement.

### 1.4 Separation of Roles

A person may simultaneously serve as Member, Founder, Officer, employee, contractor, advisor, or director if a board is later created. Each role carries separate rights and responsibilities. Removal from one role does not automatically remove another role unless this Agreement expressly provides otherwise.

### 1.5 Duty of Candor

Each Member and Officer shall promptly disclose information they reasonably believe could materially affect Company finances, legal compliance, intellectual property, cybersecurity, customer relationships, fundraising, acquisitions, material contracts, governance, or other significant Company matters. Intentional concealment of material information may constitute Cause.

### 1.6 Good Faith and Anti-Weaponization

No Member may exercise rights under this Agreement primarily to harm another Member, create unnecessary deadlock, prevent fair distributions, extract a personal benefit unrelated to the Company, or frustrate the purpose of this Agreement.

## Article II — Formation, Definitions, and Interpretation

### 2.1 Formation

The Company is organized as a limited liability company under the laws of the State of Washington and shall continue until dissolved under this Agreement or applicable law.

### 2.2 Business Purpose

The Company may engage in any lawful business activity for which a Washington limited liability company may be organized. The Members expect the Company's products, services, technologies, markets, and business model may evolve over time without requiring amendment solely because the business focus changes.

### 2.3 Principal Office and Registered Agent

The Company shall maintain a principal office and registered agent as required by law. Operational offices may be established or changed by the authorized Officer or manager within the approved operating plan.

### 2.4 Fiscal Year

Unless otherwise approved, the Company's fiscal year ends December 31.

### 2.5 Core Definitions

- **Agreement** means this Operating Agreement, including schedules, policies, exhibits, and amendments.
- **Annual Operating Plan** means the approved annual budget, operating plan, hiring plan, expense plan, reserve target, and strategic execution plan.
- **Annual Strategic Plan** means the approved statement of mission, product strategy, target customers, revenue strategy, risk tolerance, hiring roadmap, and major initiatives.
- **Approved Leave** means parental leave, medical leave, military service, caregiving leave, bereavement, or other leave approved under this Agreement.
- **Bad Leaver** means a departing Member whose departure results from Cause, abandonment, intentional IP misappropriation, fraud, theft, competitive misconduct, or other circumstances designated as Bad Leaver events.
- **Cap Table** means the Company's official ownership record.
- **Cause** means fraud, theft, embezzlement, willful misconduct, material breach not cured after notice, abandonment, misappropriation of Company IP, intentional violation of confidentiality obligations, or conduct causing material harm to the Company.
- **Company IP** means all intellectual property owned by or assigned to the Company, including code, models, prompts, workflows, datasets, documentation, customer lists, products, designs, marks, domains, trade secrets, know-how, inventions, and business materials.
- **Continuous Service** means ongoing service as a Founder, employee, officer, contractor, or other service provider, subject to Approved Leave.
- **Excess Cash** means cash exceeding current liabilities, approved reserves, tax obligations, approved operating expenditures, and reasonably anticipated strategic commitments.
- **Founder** means an original Member of the Company.
- **Good Leaver** means a departing Member whose departure results from voluntary resignation, death, disability, removal without Cause, mutual separation, or approved circumstances not constituting Cause.
- **Material Participation** means regular and meaningful participation consistent with the current Founder Roles Schedule, subject to Approved Leave.
- **Member** means a holder of Membership Interests.
- **Membership Interest** means economic and governance rights associated with ownership of the Company.
- **Membership Units** means the units representing Membership Interests.
- **Protected Approval** means approval by Members holding at least 75% of outstanding voting interests and, while all three original Founders remain Members, approval of each original Founder.
- **Reserved Matter** means a decision requiring enhanced approval under this Agreement or the Governance Matrix.
- **Strategic Technology Decision** means a technology decision that materially affects the Company's risk profile, ownership or licensing of Company IP, business model, regulatory obligations, long-term capital commitments, or technology strategy in a manner not reasonably reversible without significant cost or disruption.
- **Unanimous Approval** means approval of all voting Members entitled to vote.

### 2.6 Interpretation

Headings are for convenience only. Singular includes plural. Electronic records and signatures have the same effect as paper records and manual signatures. Where two reasonable interpretations exist, preference should be given to the interpretation that preserves Company continuity, protects Company assets, and best reflects the express governance design of this Agreement.

## Article III — Capitalization and Ownership

### 3.1 Authorized Units

The Company is authorized to issue 100,000,000 Membership Units. Authorized Units have no economic meaning until issued and recorded in the Cap Table.

### 3.2 Initial Ownership

The initial Founders shall receive equal Membership Units, intended to represent equal ownership and equal voting power at formation, subject to reverse vesting and the Company's repurchase rights. The initial Cap Table is set forth in the Initial Cap Table Schedule.

### 3.3 Official Cap Table and Equity Ledger

The Company shall maintain an official Cap Table and Equity Ledger showing issued Units, vested Units, unvested Units, repurchased Units, transfers, cancellations, reserved Units, and any equity incentive pool. No Membership Units exist unless authorized under this Agreement, entered in the Cap Table, and recorded in the Equity Ledger.

### 3.4 Reverse Vesting

Founder Units are issued immediately but are subject to the Company's repurchase right. Founder Units vest over four years with a one-year cliff and monthly vesting thereafter, beginning on the vesting commencement date stated in the Reverse Vesting Schedule.

### 3.5 Cliff and Monthly Vesting

If a Founder ceases Continuous Service before completing twelve months, 100% of that Founder's Units remain unvested and subject to repurchase. Upon the first anniversary of the vesting commencement date, 25% of Founder Units vest. The remaining 75% vests monthly over the following 36 months.

### 3.6 Company Repurchase Right

If Continuous Service terminates, the Company may repurchase all unvested Units for the original purchase price or nominal value stated in the Reverse Vesting Schedule. Vested Units are not subject to this reverse-vesting repurchase right except as otherwise provided for Good Leaver, Bad Leaver, death, disability, buy-sell, transfer, or dispute provisions.

### 3.7 Vesting During Approved Leave

Approved Leave under six consecutive months does not suspend vesting. Leave longer than six months requires a written agreement addressing responsibilities, compensation, vesting, and expected return date.

### 3.8 Double-Trigger Acceleration

If the Company is sold and, within twelve months after closing, a Founder is terminated without Cause or resigns for Good Reason as defined in the applicable transaction documents, 100% of that Founder's remaining unvested Founder Units shall vest unless otherwise approved by Protected Approval.

### 3.9 Fully Diluted Ownership

References to ownership percentages mean ownership on a fully diluted basis unless expressly stated otherwise, taking into account issued Units, reserved equity pools, and convertible instruments when applicable.

## Article IV — Governance, Voting, and Decision Procedures

### 4.1 Governance Model

Members govern ownership, capital structure, strategic direction, and Reserved Matters. Officers and other authorized Company representatives may manage ordinary-course matters and assigned responsibilities according to this Agreement, the Governance Matrix, approved plans, approved budgets, and any written delegation of authority.

### 4.2 Approval Levels

The approval levels are:

1. **Delegated Officer Authority**: decisions delegated to an Officer or other authorized Company representative under this Agreement, the Governance Matrix, approved plans, approved budgets, or written Member approval.
2. **Member Approval**: approval by Members holding more than 50% of voting interests.
3. **Protected Approval**: approval by Members holding at least 75% of voting interests and, while all three original Founders remain Members, each original Founder.
4. **Unanimous Approval**: approval of all voting Members entitled to vote.

### 4.3 Governance Matrix

The Governance Matrix controls approval thresholds unless an explicit provision of this Agreement requires a higher threshold. If a decision is not listed, an authorized Officer or Company representative may decide in the ordinary course unless the decision materially affects ownership, Company IP, financial risk, legal exposure, or long-term strategy.

### 4.4 Notice Requirements

Routine Member meetings require at least 14 calendar days' notice. Special meetings require at least 5 business days' notice. Emergency meetings require as much notice as reasonably practical. Notices must include the meeting purpose, proposed decisions, relevant materials, voting deadline if applicable, and approval threshold.

### 4.5 Voting Windows

Routine matters shall remain open for at least 7 calendar days after notice. Reserved Matters shall remain open for at least 14 calendar days. Strategic transactions, financing, acquisitions, entity conversion, or sale of substantially all assets shall remain open for at least 30 calendar days unless an external deadline reasonably requires a shorter period.

### 4.6 Silence Not Approval

Failure to vote, failure to respond, or silence does not constitute approval. A Member may abstain, approve, reject, or request additional information. Abstention counts only as abstention and not as approval.

### 4.7 Electronic Voting

Votes may be conducted by email, electronic signature platform, secure board portal, or Company-approved collaboration system if archived. Votes must identify the action approved, approval threshold, date, and voting Members.

### 4.8 Continuing Operations if Annual Plan Is Not Approved

If a new Annual Operating Plan is not approved before the start of a fiscal year, the prior year's plan continues temporarily, adjusted for inflation, contractual obligations, legal requirements, and necessary ordinary-course operations, until a replacement plan is approved or the dispute is resolved.

### 4.9 Duty to Explain Reserved Matter Objections

Before voting against a Reserved Matter, a Member shall provide a written explanation of the business reasons for the objection if requested by another Member. The explanation need not disclose privileged advice or private personal information but should be sufficient to allow meaningful discussion.

## Article V — Officers and Delegated Authority

### 5.1 Officers

The Company may appoint a CEO, CTO, COO, CFO, CIO, CAIO, CPO, CXO, CBO, President, Secretary, Treasurer, or other officers. Initial officers are listed in the Founder Roles Schedule and Officer Roles Schedule.

### 5.2 Delegation of Authority

Officers have the authority expressly delegated to them by this Agreement, the Governance Matrix, the Founder Roles Schedule, the Officer Roles Schedule, the Annual Operating Plan, approved budgets, written Member approval, or other approved Company records. No Officer may override Reserved Matters or take actions requiring Member Approval, Protected Approval, or Unanimous Approval unless that approval has been obtained.

### 5.3 Role Descriptions

Officer titles, reporting relationships, operational responsibilities, consultation expectations, and domain-specific duties should be described in the Officer Roles Schedule, Executive Handbook, Founder Roles Schedule, employment agreement, consulting agreement, board or Member resolution, or other approved Company record. Those documents may describe operational responsibilities without amending this Agreement unless they change ownership rights, voting rights, approval thresholds, vesting, buy-sell rights, or other rights expressly protected by this Agreement.

### 5.4 Limits on Officer Authority

An Officer's authority is limited by approved budgets, approved plans, applicable law, fiduciary obligations, this Agreement, the Governance Matrix, customer contractual obligations, Company policies, and Reserved Matter approval requirements. A matter must be escalated for the required approval if it materially affects ownership, Company IP, financial risk, legal exposure, customer data rights, long-term strategy, or another Reserved Matter.

### 5.5 Officer Removal

An Officer may be removed by resignation, mutual agreement, Cause, Persistent Material Non-Performance, restructuring, or the approval required in the Governance Matrix. Removal from an officer role does not affect Membership, vested equity, or voting rights unless separately provided.

### 5.6 Founder Reviews

The Founders shall conduct semi-annual Founder Reviews and update the Founder Roles Schedule. Performance concerns may affect responsibilities, officer roles, compensation, bonuses, support, or future equity grants. Performance concerns alone do not affect vested equity except where conduct independently qualifies as a Bad Leaver event.

## Article VI — Economics, Capital, and Distributions

### 6.1 Capital Contributions

Initial capital contributions are stated in the Capital Contributions Schedule. No Member is required to make additional capital contributions unless approved as required by this Agreement.

### 6.2 Founder Loans

Founder loans must be documented in writing, approved as required, and recorded in the Founder Loans Schedule. Unless otherwise approved, founder loans do not convert to equity, are subordinate to ordinary business operations and third-party debt, and bear interest at the Wall Street Journal Prime Rate minus 1%, or another rate approved in writing.

### 6.3 Allocations

Profits, losses, and tax items shall be allocated among Members in proportion to ownership interests unless otherwise required by tax law or approved in the governing documents.

### 6.4 Tax Distributions

The Company shall make tax distributions sufficient to allow each Member to pay estimated federal and applicable state income taxes attributable to Company profits allocated to that Member, subject to available cash and legal restrictions. Tax distributions are treated as advances against future distributions.

### 6.5 Operating Reserve

The Company shall maintain a reasonable operating reserve sufficient for current obligations, contractual commitments, approved hiring, approved capital expenditures, anticipated tax obligations, and a prudent contingency reserve. If no Annual Operating Plan is approved, the default reserve equals twelve months of trailing average monthly operating expenses.

### 6.6 Excess Cash and Profit Distributions

Excess Cash is presumed distributable. At least annually, the authorized Officer or finance owner shall prepare a capital allocation recommendation. Unless otherwise approved, at least 50% of Excess Cash shall be distributed to Members in proportion to ownership interests, and the remainder may be retained for approved business purposes. Retaining earnings solely to prevent Member distributions is prohibited.

### 6.7 Compensation Separate From Ownership

Founder salaries, bonuses, consulting fees, and expense reimbursements are compensation for current service and do not alter ownership unless expressly approved through the equity provisions of this Agreement.

## Article VII — Member Rights and Responsibilities

### 7.1 Information Rights

Each Member has the right to timely access to material Company information, including financial statements, budgets, Cap Table, major contracts, tax filings, litigation, IP records, cybersecurity incidents, fundraising discussions, acquisition offers, and governance records.

### 7.2 Inspection Rights

Each Member may inspect Company books and records upon reasonable notice during normal business hours, subject to confidentiality, privilege, customer contractual obligations, and data protection requirements.

### 7.3 Participation and Economic Rights

Members may participate in Member meetings, vote as provided in this Agreement, receive required notices, propose agenda items, receive minutes, receive tax distributions, receive approved distributions, and receive liquidation proceeds according to ownership interests.

### 7.4 Due Process

No Member shall lose ownership rights except through voluntary transfer, Company repurchase rights, buy-sell procedures, judicial order, or express provisions of this Agreement. Where applicable, Members are entitled to written notice, opportunity to respond, cure period, and dispute resolution.

### 7.5 Duties

Members shall protect Company confidential information, cooperate with Company filings and transactions, avoid undisclosed conflicts, protect Company property, communicate professionally, and exercise rights in good faith.

## Article VIII — Founder Departure, Leave, and Buy-Sell Mechanics

### 8.1 Good Leaver

A Good Leaver keeps vested Units and forfeits or sells unvested Units under the reverse vesting repurchase right. The Company may purchase vested Units at Fair Market Value under the buyout process.

### 8.2 Bad Leaver

A Bad Leaver forfeits or sells unvested Units under the repurchase right. The Company may purchase vested Units at Fair Market Value less 25%, to the extent enforceable, without limiting other remedies.

### 8.3 Abandonment

A Founder commits abandonment if they fail to materially perform assigned responsibilities for 90 consecutive days, receive written notice identifying the failure, and fail to cure within 30 days. Approved Leave does not constitute abandonment.

### 8.4 Persistent Material Non-Performance

If a Founder materially fails to perform responsibilities identified in the Founder Roles Schedule despite repeated reviews and cure periods over 12 consecutive months, the Founder may be removed from officer roles under the Governance Matrix. Such removal does not affect vested equity unless the conduct also constitutes Cause or a Bad Leaver event.

### 8.5 Disability and Death

Disability exists if a Founder is unable to perform core duties for 180 consecutive days or 240 days in any 12-month period. Upon death or disability, heirs or representatives receive economic rights only unless admitted as voting Members under this Agreement. The Company has the first option to buy the affected Membership Interest at Fair Market Value.

### 8.6 Buyout Priority

Upon a triggering departure, the Company has the first option to purchase available Units. If the Company declines, the remaining Founders or Members have the second option. Outside transfer is permitted only after the Company and remaining Members decline and all transfer restrictions are satisfied.

### 8.7 Valuation

Fair Market Value is determined first by mutual agreement within 15 days. If no agreement is reached, a neutral independent appraiser shall determine value. Appraisal costs are shared equally unless the departing Member is a Bad Leaver, in which case the Bad Leaver pays unless otherwise determined by the arbitrator or court.

### 8.8 Payment Terms

Unless otherwise approved, buyout payments may be made 20% at closing and the balance over 36 months with commercially reasonable interest, subject to acceleration for default.

## Article IX — Equity, Future Members, Transfers, and Exit Rights

### 9.1 Future Equity

The Company may issue additional Units, profits interests, equity incentives, or other ownership-linked rights only under the Governance Matrix and with written dilution disclosure showing valuation, units issued, price, resulting Cap Table, and voting effects.

### 9.2 Equity Incentive Plan

The Company may establish an equity incentive plan of up to 10% of fully diluted ownership upon required approval. No pool exists until formally adopted. The plan may use LLC profits interests or another equity mechanism appropriate to the Company's legal form.

### 9.3 Preemptive Rights

Before issuing new voting ownership, existing Members shall have a reasonable opportunity to purchase their pro rata share unless waived under the approval threshold required by this Agreement.

### 9.4 Transfers and ROFR

No Member may sell, assign, pledge, gift, encumber, or transfer Membership Interests without complying with this Agreement. The Company has a right of first refusal, followed by the non-transferring Members. No transferee becomes a voting Member without required approval.

### 9.5 Tag-Along Rights

If a Member proposes to sell a significant portion of Membership Interests to a third party, other Members may participate on the same terms proportionate to ownership, subject to customary exceptions.

### 9.6 Drag-Along Rights

If a sale of the Company or substantially all assets is approved under the Governance Matrix, all Members shall cooperate and participate on the same economic terms, subject to customary limitations, equal treatment of same-class Units, and preservation of indemnity protections.

## Article X — Intellectual Property, Confidentiality, and Data

### 10.1 Present Assignment

Each Founder hereby assigns to the Company all Company IP created, conceived, authored, developed, reduced to practice, generated, trained, collected, configured, or improved for the Company, within the Company's business, using Company resources, or within the scope of Company work, including pre-formation work performed for the benefit of the Company.

### 10.2 AI Assets

Company IP includes prompts, prompt libraries, agent workflows, RAG pipelines, evaluation frameworks, model outputs, fine-tunes, embeddings, synthetic datasets, datasets, model documentation, AI observability systems, and derivative works created for the Company.

### 10.3 Pre-Existing IP

Each Founder shall list pre-existing intellectual property in the Pre-Existing IP Schedule. IP not listed and used in Company work is presumed assigned or licensed to the Company to the fullest extent necessary for the Company to operate and commercialize its business.

### 10.4 Confidentiality

Members and Officers shall protect Company confidential information during and after association with the Company. Trade secret obligations survive indefinitely. Business confidential information survives for at least five years unless a longer period applies by law or contract.

### 10.5 Return of Property

Upon departure or request, a Member shall return Company devices, documents, credentials, tokens, API keys, source code, datasets, customer information, and other Company property within 10 business days, and certify deletion of Company data from personal systems where legally and technically feasible.

### 10.6 Non-Solicitation

For 12 months after departure, a departing Member may not knowingly solicit Company employees, contractors, active customers, or active prospects for a competitive purpose, to the extent enforceable under applicable law.

### 10.7 Open Source

No Founder, employee, or contractor may release Company code, prompt libraries, proprietary datasets, model weights, proprietary AI tooling, or internal workflows to open source without required approval. Use of open-source software is permitted subject to license review. Restrictive licenses affecting proprietary code require approval.

## Article XI — AI Governance

### 11.1 Governance Principle

The Agreement governs business risk, not routine engineering implementation. Ordinary AI implementation decisions may be made under delegated authority. Strategic Technology Decisions require approval under the Governance Matrix.

### 11.2 Customer Data

Customer data belongs to the customer or is controlled by customer contract. The Company may use customer data only as permitted by customer agreement, Company policy, and applicable law. Customer data may not be used to train general-purpose models, benchmark third-party models, or create resale datasets unless expressly permitted.

### 11.3 AI Assets Register

The Company shall maintain an AI Assets Register tracking material models, fine-tunes, prompt libraries, datasets, external APIs, training sources, licenses, evaluation systems, and major AI workflows.

### 11.4 Model Provenance

The Company shall maintain reasonable documentation of material training data sources, third-party models, licensing obligations, evaluation criteria, and model-use restrictions relevant to Company products.

### 11.5 Strategic Technology Decisions

Strategic Technology Decisions include developing a proprietary foundation model, releasing proprietary model weights, selling or exclusively licensing Core Technology, selling strategic datasets, entering regulated AI markets, committing to material AI infrastructure investments above approved thresholds, or materially changing customer data usage rights.

## Article XII — Financial Controls, Records, and Insurance

### 12.1 Books and Records

The Company shall maintain accurate books, financial records, tax records, Cap Table, Equity Ledger, governance records, material contracts, IP records, and AI Assets Register.

### 12.2 Quarterly Reporting

The authorized Officer or finance owner shall provide quarterly reports including profit and loss, balance sheet, cash flow, budget versus actual, Cap Table changes, and KPI summary.

### 12.3 Banking Controls

An authorized Officer may open bank accounts and approve routine payments within approved budgets. Transfers under $25,000 may be approved by an authorized Officer. Transfers from $25,000 to $100,000 require Member Approval or approval of at least two founders while there are three Founders. Transfers above $100,000 or outside approved budgets require the approval stated in the Governance Matrix.

### 12.4 Signing Authority

An authorized Officer may sign ordinary-course contracts within approved thresholds. Contracts involving equity, debt, M&A, exclusive licensing of Core Technology, sale of Company IP, material customer data rights, or material strategic commitments require approval under the Governance Matrix.

### 12.5 Insurance

The Company may purchase D&O, E&O, cyber liability, commercial general liability, key-person insurance, or other insurance when appropriate. Policies above an annual premium threshold approved in the Annual Operating Plan require Member Approval.

## Article XIII — Dispute Resolution and Deadlock

### 13.1 Good-Faith Negotiation

Members shall first attempt in good faith to resolve disputes through direct negotiation for 30 days unless emergency relief is required.

### 13.2 Mediation

If unresolved, the dispute proceeds to mediation with a Washington-based mediator jointly selected by the parties. If the parties cannot agree on a mediator within 10 business days, a mediator shall be selected under AAA or similar procedures.

### 13.3 Arbitration

If mediation fails, disputes shall be resolved by binding arbitration under commercially reasonable arbitration rules. The arbitrator may award equitable relief, damages, specific performance, and other remedies permitted by law and this Agreement.

### 13.4 Bad-Faith Fee Shifting

Each party bears its own attorneys' fees and costs unless the arbitrator or court finds that a party acted in bad faith, committed fraud, concealed material information, or pursued frivolous claims or defenses. In such cases, the prevailing party may recover reasonable fees and costs.

### 13.5 Shotgun Deadlock

For unresolved ownership or control deadlocks after negotiation, mediation, and arbitration, any Founder may issue a buy-sell notice stating a Company valuation and purchase price for the sender's interest. The recipient Members must elect within 30 days either to sell at that valuation or buy the sender's interest at that valuation. Closing occurs within 90 days unless otherwise approved.

### 13.6 If No Founder Can Fund Buyout

If no Founder can reasonably complete a shotgun purchase, the Company shall be marketed for sale to third parties for 180 days unless the Members approve another resolution.

### 13.7 Emergency Relief

A party may seek temporary or emergency injunctive relief to protect Company IP, confidential information, customer data, trade secrets, or Company assets without first completing mediation or arbitration.

## Article XIV — Strategic Events

### 14.1 Acquisition Offers

A bona fide acquisition offer must be disclosed to all Members within 5 business days after receipt by any Officer or Member. All material documents must be shared subject to confidentiality. Members shall have at least 30 days to evaluate unless external timing reasonably requires a shorter period.

### 14.2 Financing

Institutional financing, convertible instruments, SAFEs, priced equity rounds, venture debt, or financing that may affect ownership or control require approval under the Governance Matrix.

### 14.3 Entity Conversion

The Company may convert into another entity, including a Delaware corporation, if approved under the Governance Matrix and if reasonably necessary or desirable for financing, acquisition, tax efficiency, governance, or strategic purposes.

### 14.4 Dissolution

Voluntary dissolution requires Unanimous Approval unless otherwise required by law. The Company shall be wound up in accordance with applicable law, this Agreement, and equal treatment of Members holding the same class of Units.

## Article XV — Miscellaneous

### 15.1 Notices

Official notices may be delivered by email, electronic signature platform, certified mail, secure board portal, or Company-approved collaboration system if archived. Notices are effective upon confirmed delivery or the next business day after electronic transmission unless a bounce-back or delivery failure is received.

### 15.2 Amendments

Amendments require Unanimous Approval unless this Agreement expressly permits a schedule, policy, threshold, or administrative exhibit to be updated by a lower approval level. Amendments affecting ownership percentages, voting rights, vesting, founder protections, buy-sell rights, dispute procedures, or dissolution require Unanimous Approval.

### 15.3 Spousal Consent

Each married Founder shall use reasonable efforts to obtain spousal consent acknowledging transfer restrictions, buy-sell provisions, and the separate governance treatment of Membership Interests, to the extent permitted by law.

### 15.4 No Broad Non-Compete

This Agreement does not impose a broad non-compete. It protects the Company through confidentiality, trade secret, non-solicitation, conflict, IP assignment, and fiduciary provisions.

### 15.5 Severability

If any provision is unenforceable, the remaining provisions remain effective, and the unenforceable provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable while preserving intent.

### 15.6 Entire Agreement

This Agreement, including schedules and approved policies, constitutes the complete agreement among Members regarding Company governance and supersedes prior inconsistent understandings.

### 15.7 Signature Pages

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts and by electronic signature. Each counterpart is deemed an original, and all counterparts together constitute one agreement.
