# AIExpertly Executive Handbook

Version: v0.2 draft  
Status: Founder review draft

> This handbook defines executive roles, authority, accountability, consultation duties, and expected outcomes. It supports growth by clarifying role coverage before every role is filled.

## 1. Purpose

This handbook defines executive roles, domain authority, accountability, consultation duties, succession expectations, and review cadence for AIExpertly.

It is designed to answer: who owns what, who must be consulted, and when a decision escalates from executive authority to Member governance.

## 2. Executive Governance Model

Authority follows expertise. Accountability follows organizational hierarchy. Executives have domain authority within approved budgets and plans unless a decision exceeds delegated authority, materially affects another domain, or becomes a Strategic Company Decision.

### Core Rule

Executives decide implementation. Members decide ownership, capital structure, major risk, and strategic company direction.

## 3. CEO — Chief Executive Officer

**Mission:** Maximize sustainable enterprise value and ensure effective Company execution.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- Company strategy and execution;
- Annual Strategic Plan;
- Annual Operating Plan;
- revenue, sales, partnerships, marketing, and customer success oversight;
- hiring priorities and organizational structure;
- cross-functional prioritization;
- fundraising coordination;
- external representation;
- governance communication to Members.

**Authority:** Ordinary-course business decisions within approved plans and budgets. The CEO may not change ownership, issue equity, admit Members, borrow above thresholds, sell core assets, sell the Company, amend governance rights, or materially deviate from approved strategy without required approval.

**Consults with:** CTO on technology commitments, CFO on capital allocation, CPO on product strategy, CXO on customer impact, CBO on partnerships, COO on operational capacity.

**KPIs:** revenue, enterprise value, runway, customer retention, execution velocity, strategic milestone delivery, team health, risk management.

## 4. CTO — Chief Technology Officer

**Mission:** Deliver scalable, secure, maintainable, and strategically valuable technology.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- software architecture;
- AI architecture;
- engineering organization;
- infrastructure;
- reliability;
- security engineering;
- development methodology;
- cloud architecture;
- engineering standards;
- technical roadmap;
- technical vendor selection within budget.

**Authority:** Technical implementation decisions, including model vendors, databases, frameworks, cloud tooling, CI/CD, monitoring, prompt orchestration, RAG implementation, model routing, and engineering methodology, provided the decision is within approved budget and does not materially change Company risk.

**Strategic escalation:** building proprietary foundation models, selling or exclusively licensing core IP, selling strategic datasets, committing to material infrastructure investments, entering regulated AI markets, or materially changing customer data architecture requires governance approval.

**Consults with:** CEO on business implications, CFO on major infrastructure spend, CPO on product feasibility, CAIO on AI evaluation and safety, CIO on internal systems overlap.

**KPIs:** reliability, security, engineering velocity, system scalability, incident frequency, technical debt, infrastructure cost efficiency, product quality.

## 5. COO — Chief Operating Officer

**Mission:** Build scalable operating systems that allow the Company to execute reliably.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- internal operations;
- process design;
- operating cadence;
- vendor operations;
- legal operations coordination;
- people operations administration;
- delivery and execution systems;
- cross-functional program management;
- operating metrics.

**Authority:** Operational processes, internal systems, vendor workflows, delivery mechanisms, and administrative operations within approved budgets.

**Consults with:** CEO on priorities, CFO on financial controls, CIO on internal systems, CXO on customer-facing operations.

**KPIs:** process cycle time, delivery reliability, cost efficiency, operating cadence compliance, internal SLA performance, team capacity.

## 6. CFO — Chief Financial Officer

**Mission:** Preserve financial discipline and support strategic capital allocation.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- finance;
- accounting;
- treasury;
- tax planning;
- budgets;
- financial reporting;
- capital allocation analysis;
- fundraising support;
- investor reporting;
- insurance review;
- founder loan documentation.

**Authority:** Financial policies, reporting systems, accounting vendors, budget analysis, cash management recommendations, and finance operations within approved plans.

**Consults with:** CEO on budgets and capital strategy, COO on operating systems, Members on distributions, auditors and accountants as needed.

**KPIs:** cash runway, forecast accuracy, budget variance, reporting timeliness, tax compliance, gross margin, operating margin.

## 7. CIO — Chief Information Officer

**Mission:** Ensure the Company's internal technology systems are secure, reliable, compliant, and scalable.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- internal IT;
- identity and access management;
- business applications;
- employee devices;
- enterprise security systems;
- compliance tooling;
- internal data systems;
- collaboration platforms.

**Authority:** Internal technology systems and access controls within approved plans and budgets.

**Distinction from CTO:** The CTO builds product technology. The CIO runs internal technology.

**Consults with:** CTO on security architecture overlap, COO on internal operations, CFO on vendor spend, CEO on business continuity.

**KPIs:** access review completion, device compliance, internal system uptime, employee onboarding efficiency, audit readiness.

## 8. CAIO — Chief AI Officer

**Mission:** Maximize the Company's competitive advantage through responsible, measurable, and reliable AI capabilities.

**Reporting relationship:** The CAIO reports to the CTO unless otherwise approved.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- AI governance;
- AI experimentation;
- model evaluation;
- prompt engineering standards;
- AI safety practices;
- responsible AI processes;
- AI observability;
- benchmarking;
- AI Assets Register;
- internal AI enablement;
- model provenance documentation.

**Authority:** AI standards, evaluation approaches, prompt practices, benchmark processes, and AI governance implementation within technical strategy.

**Limits:** The CAIO does not independently decide Company strategy, customer data usage rights, sale of AI assets, or strategic technology commitments.

**Consults with:** CTO on architecture, CPO on product behavior, CXO on customer-facing AI impacts, CEO on business risk, legal/security advisors where appropriate.

**KPIs:** model quality, evaluation coverage, cost per task, hallucination/error rates, safety incidents, benchmark reliability, AI capability adoption.

## 9. CPO — Chief Product Officer

**Mission:** Build products customers value and the business can scale.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- product vision;
- product roadmap;
- customer research;
- product requirements;
- UX quality;
- prioritization;
- product metrics;
- launch readiness;
- customer value validation.

**Authority:** Product roadmap and feature prioritization within the Annual Strategic Plan and Annual Operating Plan.

**Consults with:** CEO on business priorities, CTO on feasibility and technical tradeoffs, CXO on customer experience, CBO on partner commitments.

**KPIs:** activation, retention, feature adoption, customer satisfaction, roadmap delivery, product-market fit evidence.

## 10. CXO — Chief Experience Officer

**Mission:** Make customers successful and preserve trust across the customer lifecycle.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- onboarding;
- support;
- customer success;
- retention;
- customer health;
- advocacy;
- NPS or equivalent metrics;
- renewal support;
- customer feedback loops.

**Authority:** Customer experience operations, support standards, onboarding workflows, and retention playbooks within approved plans.

**Consults with:** CPO on product friction, CTO on reliability, CAIO on AI behavior affecting customers, CEO on escalation and strategic accounts.

**KPIs:** retention, churn, support response time, onboarding completion, customer health, expansion opportunities, customer satisfaction.

## 11. CBO — Chief Business Officer

**Mission:** Develop strategic business opportunities that expand the Company's reach and enterprise value.

**Primary responsibilities:**

- partnerships;
- strategic alliances;
- corporate development;
- ecosystem relationships;
- licensing strategy;
- business development;
- channel opportunities;
- acquisition scouting support.

**Authority:** Business development and partnership exploration within approved strategy. Binding commitments that materially affect IP, exclusivity, revenue model, customer commitments, or financial risk require escalation.

**Consults with:** CEO on strategy, CFO on financial terms, CTO on technology commitments, CPO on product commitments, legal counsel on exclusivity or licensing.

**KPIs:** partner pipeline, strategic revenue, qualified opportunities, ecosystem leverage, corporate development outcomes.

## 12. Consultation Rules

Executives must consult affected domains before decisions that materially affect those domains. Consultation is not a veto unless the Governance Matrix requires approval.

Examples:

- CTO consults CFO before major infrastructure commitments.
- CEO consults CTO before customer commitments requiring major engineering work.
- CPO consults CXO before major onboarding or support changes.
- CBO consults legal/CEO before exclusive partnerships.
- CAIO consults CPO and CXO when AI behavior affects product or customer experience.

## 13. Escalation Rules

Escalate to the CEO when:

- a decision materially affects multiple domains;
- executives disagree and the issue is not a Reserved Matter;
- resources must be reallocated across teams;
- customer commitments materially affect roadmap or engineering work.

Escalate to Member governance when:

- ownership, voting, or capital structure is affected;
- Company IP may be sold or exclusively licensed;
- major debt or financing is involved;
- customer data rights materially change;
- strategic technology commitments materially change Company risk;
- the decision is listed in the Governance Matrix.

## 14. Succession

Each executive should maintain a succession note identifying:

- critical responsibilities;
- key systems;
- current projects;
- delegated owners;
- emergency backup;
- open risks.

The CEO role should have an interim succession plan reviewed annually. Critical operational domains should have named interim coverage once the Company has sufficient personnel.

## 15. Role Review

Executive roles should be reviewed annually and whenever the Company materially changes size, business model, funding status, product strategy, or risk profile.

The role review should answer:

1. Is this role still needed?
2. Is the authority clear?
3. Is the reporting relationship clear?
4. Are there domain conflicts?
5. Are KPIs useful?
6. Is succession coverage adequate?

## 16. Initial Coverage Before Roles Are Filled

Until a role is filled, the CEO may assign interim coverage. If no assignment is made:

- CEO covers COO, CFO, CXO, and CBO responsibilities by default.
- CTO covers CIO and CAIO responsibilities by default.
- Product responsibilities are assigned by the CEO in consultation with the CTO.

Interim coverage should be documented in the Officer Roles Schedule.
